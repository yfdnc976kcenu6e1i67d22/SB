Patience, not instant gratification – It takes months and years to develop a distance runner. Instant success is rare. Hard work – There are no shortcuts to success. The magnitude of the rewards are proportional to the effort that is put into the sport. Intrinsic rewards – Unfortunately, many times there is little public recognition given to even the elite performers in cross-country; however, the satisfaction that an athlete receives if he or she is truly passionate is immeasurable. Extrinsic rewards should not be neglected, but they cannot become the primary focus if long-term success is to be achieved in this, the loneliest of sports. Responsibility for personal health – The body is a temple, and you are given only one; therefore it is paramount that we take care of it. Eating correctly, getting the proper amount of rest, and drinking fluids are important to success in cross-country, and they are some of the key ingredients to living a healthy life.
